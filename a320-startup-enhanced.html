Stage 2: Safety Checks Before Applying Power

Why We Do This

Before we turn on electrical power, we must verify the aircraft is in a safe state. If certain switches are in the wrong position, applying power could cause equipment to start moving or activate unexpectedly, which could damage the aircraft or injure ground crew.

Step 1Check Battery Voltage

Click on BATTERY 1 and BATTERY 2 below. The voltage should read above 25.5V on both. This ensures we have enough battery power to start systems.

Stage 3: Power On Sequence

Power-Up Procedure

We'll now bring the aircraft to life in a specific sequence. The order matters!

Step 1Turn Batteries to AUTO

Click BATTERY 1, then BATTERY 2 to set them to AUTO. This connects the batteries to the electrical system.

Step 2Connect External Power

Click EXT POWER ON to connect ground power. You'll see systems start to power up.

Step 3Start APU

Click APU MASTER, wait 3 seconds, then click APU START. The APU (Auxiliary Power Unit) is a small jet engine in the tail that provides electrical power and air.
